NASA Summer School Topic 5:

Sensitivity of Equilibrium Climate to Physical Parameterizations


Goal: Study the role of different physical parameterizations in climate simulation and climate change response.

DOWNLOAD PROJECT PDF

Project Description

Topic: Sensitivity of Equilibrium Climate to Physical Parameterizations

Background:

  1. Physical parameterizations are responsible for large part of uncertainty of climate change sensitivity.
  2. Understanding the role of different physical parameterizations can help focus model development.

Datasets:

  • 16 (8 control + 8 climate change), 12-month averaged WRF aqua-planet simulations with different combinations of physical parameterizations.
  • Earth-like aqua-planet simulations (prescribed SST, no annual cycle, zonally symmetric forcing/results)
  • Climate change simulations – uniform 4K increase of SST
  • Variables covered:
    1. Horizontal and vertical velocity components
    2. Cloud cover
    3. Water vapor and temperature profiles
    4. Precipitation
    5. Radiative fluxes at the top of the atmosphere

Questions:

  1. Is aqua-planet climate similar to (good approximation for) real climate?
  2. Quantify the role of physical parameterizations for the control climate.
    1. Which climate regions, physical quantities are most sensitive to physical parameterizations?
    2. To which physical parameterizations are they sensitive?
  3. Investigate the role of physical parameterizations in simulated “climate change”.
    1. How do different atmospheric properties change with increase of SST?
    2. How sensitive are these changes to physical parameterizations?

Tasks:

  1. Familiarize yourself with the available data sets.
  2. Study the simple examples provided in the product document.
  3. Formulate your own hypothesis.
  4. Use the available data to verify and quantify your hypothesis.
  5. Make a story based on your results.
