NASA Summer School Topic 4: Land Water Storage Variability

Goals

To study land water storage and soil moisture variability as a function of human and natural controls.

Project Description

Topic: Land water storage variability as a function of human and natural controls

Datasets:

  • GRACE water storage
  • TRMM/GPM precipitation
  • AIRS/ECMWF global near surface air temperature
  • GLDAS/SMAP soil moisture

Geographic foci: Global, Northern India, Southwest US

Questions:

  1. Is there an apparent seasonality in GRACE water storage, GLDAS model soil moisture, or SMAP soil moisture in heavily managed (agricultural) regions? How does that seasonality temporally align with seasonality in precipitation and near-surface temperature, and align with the agricultural seasons when soil water is used for irrigation?
  2. Is there longer-term variability in GRACE time series that are not represented in precipitation, temperature or soil moisture (e.g. inter-annual & trend)?
  3. From these datasets, what conclusions can we draw regarding the natural or anthropogenic drivers of water storage and soil moisture in this system at different time scales? Can these datasets be used to identify and quantify human influences on the water cycle?
